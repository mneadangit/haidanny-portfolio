A fully autonomous feeding system that doesn't just schedule meals — it sees, thinks, and confirms.

The Problem

Standard smart feeders are time-based and blind. They dispense food even when the pet isn't there, overfeed, double-feed, or completely miss the pet's presence. There's no intelligence — just a timer.

🤖

The Solution

Motion triggers a camera snapshot. Google Gemini AI analyzes the image to confirm it's your pet. Only then does the feeder dispense the right portion — then a second AI call confirms the kibble actually dropped. Full loop, zero guessing.

Full Feature Breakdown

Everything This Automation Does

Click any feature to expand the full details. This is what makes it the smartest pet feeder on the planet.

The automation doesn't trigger blindly on motion. It uses AI vision to verify your actual pet is at the bowl, ignoring shadows, robot vacuums, or passing humans.

💡 Pro Tip — Write a descriptive prompt

"Analyze this security camera footage. I am looking for a black cat. Look for silhouettes, glowing eyes (IR reflection), or a black shape on the floor/furniture. Ignore motionless objects. Respond ONLY with exactly one of these two strings: 'black_cat_detected: true' or 'black_cat_detected: false'"

After dropping food, the system takes a second photo and uses AI to visually verify that the kibble actually made it into the bowl. No guessing. The blueprint comes with a predefined input you can customize based on your bowl setup.

If the automation triggers but the AI sees an empty bowl, it instantly sends a critical alert to your phone with the photo. The smartest part: it does not count the grams, and it does not reset the 2-hour feeding timer. Because the AI couldn't confirm the food, HA treats it as if the meal never happened.

If the device loses power or Wi-Fi, the system instantly sends an offline alert. It then follows up with a "back online" notification the exact second it reconnects.

To prevent alert spam, the automation locks itself for 5 minutes after running. If your pet begs during those 5 minutes, nothing happens. If they come back after the 5-minute window, the AI realizes they still haven't eaten and will automatically try to feed them again.

Tracks every single gram fed throughout the day. You set your pet's exact daily limit in the setup menu (pre-filled at 104g as an example). If your pet hits that daily limit, the kitchen is safely closed until tomorrow.

Pets are usually hungriest right when they wake up and right before bed. The automation drops a double portion during early morning and late evening and sticks to single portions during the day. Fully configurable.

Ever wonder why a smart home automation didn't trigger? You don't have to guess. The AI writes plain-English notes directly into your Home Assistant Activity feed — like "Pet not fed! Ate recently" or "False alarm. AI rejected image" — giving you complete visibility into its decision-making.

  • ✓ Every successful feeding sends a notification with a photo of the bowl + daily gram progress report
  • ✓ Nighttime lockout — set exact start/end times, feeder goes to sleep so you aren't woken up
  • ✓ Customizable anti-double-feeding cooldown timer (e.g., block for 2 hours after last meal)
  • ✓ "Skip-A-Meal" memory — tell the system to skip the next meal without touching the main code
